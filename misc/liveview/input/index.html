Event Details Beginning at 9:00AM today, Sunday the 18th January, the UNSW Solar Racing Team's world record holding solar car, Sunswift III, will complete a lap of honour around greater Sydney and across the Harbour Bridge.

Beginning at 9:00am today, Sunday the 18th January, the UNSW Solar Racing Team's world record holding solar car, Sunswift III, will be completing a lap of honour around greater Sydney and across the Harbour Bridge.

This 110km drive aims to demonstrate the potential of solar electric transport and marks Sunswift III's final journey and retirement from competitive racing. Furthermore, this event commemorates the launch of the team's campaign for the 2009 World Solar Challenge with a brand new solar racing vehicle, Sunswift IV.

For the past 14 years, the UNSW Solar Racing Team has been on the forefront of technical innovation and cutting edge vehicle design. Sunswift III has contributed to many of the team’s achievements and successes: the completion of two World Solar Challenges, an Engineering Excellence Award for Education and Training and the CSIRO Technical Innovation Award from the World Solar Challenge 2007. Additionally, Sunswift III holds the current Transcontinental Record, which it broke by three days in 2007.

With this legacy behind it, Sunswift IV's design incorporates upright seating, the development of complex race strategy software and a steering wheel with integrated driver controls (previous cars had push-pull steering). This aims to move away from space-age like solar cars, allowing for more passenger practicality.

This historic drive will finish in the Royal Botanical Gardens (enter at the QE II Gate, next to the Opera House) at approximately 11am, where the car will be welcomed by NSW Chief Scientist Prof. Mary O'Kane This is your last chance to catch Sunswift III on the streets and sneak a preview of Sunswift IV's new vehicle design.

Below it, the speed of the car is documented with respect to various times of Sunswift III's final journey. All information is broadcast live and directly from Sunswift III on the road.

For more information about the team, please see www.sunswift.net.
